Method of calculating OTS spectrum

Zhao's method is,
$$ P_{s} d\Omega d\omega=\frac{1}{2 \pi} I_{0} r_{e}^{2} V n_{e} \left(1+\frac{2 \omega}{\omega_{i}}\right) sin \left( \varphi \right)^{2} S \left( \vec{k},\omega \right) d\Omega d\omega \tag{a1} $$ which described the spectrum of radiation caused by the incoming lights. The shape of spectrum was primarily determined by \(S(k,\omega)\), which is,
$$ S \left( \vec{k},\omega \right)=\frac{2 \pi}{k} \left|1-\frac{\chi_{e}}{\epsilon}\right|^{2} f_{e0} \left( \frac{\omega}{k} \right)+\frac{2 \pi}{k} \left|\frac{\chi_{e}}{\epsilon}\right|^{2} \sum_{j}{\frac{Z_{j}^{2} N_{j}}{\sum_{i}{Z_{i}^{2} N_{i}}} f_{i0, j} \left( \frac{\omega}{k} \right)} \tag{a2} $$ $$ \chi_{e} \left( k,\omega \right)=\alpha^{2} w \left( \omega\bigg/\left(\sqrt{2} k v_{te}\right) \right) \tag{a3} $$ $$ \chi_{j} \left( k,\omega \right)=\alpha^{2} \frac{Z_{j}^{2} N_{j}}{\sum_{j}{Z_{j} N_{j}}} \frac{T_{e}}{T_{j}} w \left( \omega\bigg/\left(\sqrt{2} k v_{tj}\right) \right) \tag{a4} $$ The above three equations are good for any collisionless plasma having arbitrary velocity distribution. When we narrow down the condition to Maxwellian distribution, three more equations comes out,
$$ f_{e0} \left( v \right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} v_{te}} exp \left( -\frac{v^{2}}{2 v_{te}^{2}} \right) \tag{a5} $$ $$ f_{i0, j} \left( v \right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} v_{t, j}} exp \left( -\frac{v^{2}}{2 v_{t, j}^{2}} \right) \tag{a6} $$ $$ w \left( x \right)=1-2 x e^{-x^{2}} \int_{0}^{x}{dp\ }{e^{p^{2}}}+i \sqrt{\pi} x e^{-x^{2}} \tag{a7} $$ Equation [7]. is only good for Maxwellian distribution.
