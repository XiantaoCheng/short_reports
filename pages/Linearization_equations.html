Linearization equations

The 1D equations are,
$$ \left(\frac{\partial^{2}}{\partial t ^{2}}+\omega_{p}^{2}+\nu_{e} \frac{\partial }{\partial t}-3 v_{B}^{2} \frac{\partial^{2}}{\partial x ^{2}}\right) n_{1}=\epsilon_{0} \omega_{p}^{2} \frac{\partial^{2}}{\partial x ^{2}} \left(A_{0} A_{1}\right) \tag{1} $$ $$ \left(\frac{\partial^{2}}{\partial t ^{2}}+\omega_{p}^{2}-c^{2} \frac{\partial^{2}}{\partial x ^{2}}\right) A_{1}=-\frac{\omega_{p}^{2}}{n_{0}} n_{1} A_{0} \tag{2} $$ The plasma density wave, \(\omega\) and k, should satisfy conditions,
$$ \omega_{0}=\omega_{1}+\omega \tag{3} $$ $$ k_{0}=k_{1}+k \tag{4} $$ Equations (1) and (2) will become,
$$ \left(-\omega^{2}+\omega_{p}^{2}-i \nu_{e} \omega+3 v_{B}^{2} k^{2}\right) n_{1}=-\epsilon_{0} \omega_{p}^{2} k^{2} \left(A_{0} A_{1}\right) \tag{5} $$ $$ \left(-\omega_{1}^{2}+\omega_{p}^{2}+c^{2} k_{1}^{2}\right) A_{1}=-\frac{\omega_{p}^{2}}{n_{0}} n_{1} A_{0} \tag{6} $$ By combining them, we will get,
$$ \left(\omega-\omega_{1}\right) \left(\omega-\omega_{2}\right) \left(\omega-\omega_{3}\right) \left(\omega-\omega_{4}\right)=c^{2} k^{2} \omega_{p}^{2} a_{0}^{2} \tag{7} $$ where,
$$ \omega_{1,2}=\omega_{0}\pm \sqrt{\omega_{p}^{2}+c^{2} \left(k_{0}-k\right)^{2}} \tag{8} $$ $$ \omega_{3,4}=-\frac{i \nu_{e}\pm \sqrt{-\nu_{e}^{2}+4 \left(\omega_{p}^{2}+3 v_{B}^{2} k^{2}\right)}}{2} \tag{9} $$
